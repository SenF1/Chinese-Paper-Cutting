Chinese Paper Cutting Ornaments

Introduction

Paper cutting is one of the most popular decorative Chinese handicrafts. It is popular for its low cost and artistry. Rural women often make paper cuttings in their spare time when farming is not busy. As a reflection of its philosophical origins, paper cutting is as comprehensive as it is attractive. Moreover, folk paper cutting conveys the content and nature of traditional culture with its own particular language.


Phoenix

The phoenix represents auspiciousness and peace, and it is precisely because the phoenix has the sacred and sublime connotation given by people.



Chinese Dragon

As the spiritual totem of the China, the Chinese dragon has been worshiped and respected by people since ancient times.


Flowers and Birds

In Chinese folk paper-cut creation, flowers and birds are eternal themes because flowers and birds themselves have beautiful meanings and carry people's good wishes.


Facial Makeup

A traditional Chinese opera performer's face painting, used for stage performance of cosmetic plastic arts.


Fish

The shape and texture of fish are widely used in traditional Chinese jewelry. They are not only vivid and exciting but also a symbol of a promising future and luck.


Plants

A symbol of tenacious life. Wish one can be as tenacious and unyielding as the grass.
